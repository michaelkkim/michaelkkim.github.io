## Education

**Ph.D. in Statistics (University of Florida)**
- Dissertation: Bayesian Methods for Estimating Optimal Biomarker Composites to Quantify Disease Progression in Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy
- Advisor: Michael J. Daniels

**B.S. in Mathematical Decision Sciences, B.A. in Mathematics (UNC Chapel Hill)**
- Mentors: Shankar Bhamidi, Iain Carmichael

I am currently a statistics Ph.D. candidate at the University of Florida, advised by Michael J. Daniels. My graduate research at the University of Florida while I was doing my Ph.D. focuses on Bayesian hierarchical modeling for incomplete longitudinal data, with applications to quantifying disease progression in Duchenne muscular dystrophy.

I received a B.S. in statistics and B.A. in Mathematics at UNC Chapel Hill and majored in statistics and mathematics. My undergrad research focused on visualizing and analyzing the citation network of the Supreme Court of the United States (SCOTUS), as well as using natural language processing to study SCOTUS text data.
